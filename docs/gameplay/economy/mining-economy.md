# Buildings: Mining economy

Mining provides **raw materials** required for tools, weapons, and gold.

## Coal Mine

### What it does
Produces **coal**.

### Key points
- Coal is used for **smelting iron and gold**.
- Coal mines consume **food** to operate.
- Coal is often the first mining bottleneck.

## Iron Mine

### What it does
Produces **iron ore**.

### Key points
- Iron ore must be smelted into **iron bars**.
- Iron is essential for tools and weapons.

## Gold Mine

### What it does
Produces **gold ore**.

### Key points
- Gold is not required for economy, only military morale.
- Gold mines are optional but useful in prolonged wars.

## Granite Mine

### What it does
Produces **granite** (stone).

### Key points
- Used only if surface granite is exhausted.
- Often inefficient compared to surface quarries.

## Mining heuristics

- Always ensure **food supply first**; starving mines stop instantly.
- Coal usually limits growth more than iron.
- Place bakeries close to mines for faster food delivery.
