# Buildings: Construction economy

The construction economy provides **boards** and **stones**, which are required for almost every building and tool.

## Lumberjack

### What it does
Cuts trees and produces **logs**.

### Key points
- Needs **trees** in range.
- Trees are a renewable resource if paired with **foresters**.
- Too many lumberjacks without foresters will eventually stall.

## Forester

### What it does
Plants **new trees**.

### Key points
- Essential for sustainable wood production.
- Should be placed so planted trees fall inside lumberjack ranges.
- Also indirectly supports **hunters** by helping forests grow.

## Sawmill

### What it does
Turns **logs → boards**.

### Key points
- Boards are required for almost all buildings and tools.
- Early bottlenecks often come from not enough sawmills.
- Place near storehouses to reduce transport delays.

## Quarry

### What it does
Extracts **stone** from nearby granite deposits.

### Key points
- Granite is **finite**.
- Once a deposit is depleted, the quarry becomes useless.
- Plan road access early, as quarries are often built on rough terrain.

## Construction economy heuristics

- Early game: prioritize **1 lumberjack + 1 forester + 1 sawmill**.
- Expand sawmill capacity early to avoid building stalls.
- Don’t overbuild quarries; stone usually lasts long enough if managed.
